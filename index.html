Background and History:

The Democratic Banana Republic was not always a democracy. In its early days, it was ruled under Pascal’s original regime, a chaotic and highly questionable fascist-style government that left little room for fairness or input from others. Over time, dissatisfaction grew among the members, particularly Andrei and Emmett, who recognized the need for change. Refusing to accept the status quo, they took action—organizing, challenging authority, and ultimately overthrowing the old system through sheer determination and effort.

In the aftermath, rather than removing Pascal entirely, they made a bold decision: to establish a new democratic government and place him back in charge, giving him the opportunity to prove himself as a fair and just leader. Meanwhile, Sahil notably chose to stay on the sidelines, observing events unfold rather than contributing to the revolution.
